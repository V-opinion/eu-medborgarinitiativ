Judaism vs Islam vs Christianity vs Hinduism: A Full Comparison

Overview of the Four Major Religions

This page compares the beliefs, history, practices, and values of Judaism, Islam, Christianity, and Hinduism to help readers understand both their differences and similarities.

Key Beliefs and Doctrines

Judaism

Judaism emphasizes a covenantal relationship with God, expressed through the Torah and Jewish law.

Islam

Islam teaches submission to Allah’s will, guided by the Qur’an and the example of Prophet Muhammad.

Christianity

Christianity centers on faith in Jesus Christ as the Son of God and salvation through His sacrifice.

Hinduism

Hinduism embraces a wide range of philosophies, focusing on karma, dharma, and reincarnation.

Sacred Texts

  • Judaism: Torah
  • Islam: Qur’an
  • Christianity: Bible
  • Hinduism: Vedas and Upanishads

Worship Practices and Rituals

Each religion has distinct forms of worship, from synagogue services in Judaism to temple rituals in Hinduism.

Holidays and Festivals

Examples include Passover (Judaism), Ramadan (Islam), Easter (Christianity), and Diwali (Hinduism).

Views on Afterlife

Judaism emphasizes moral responsibility in this life; Islam and Christianity teach eternal reward or punishment; Hinduism teaches reincarnation until liberation (moksha).

Ethical and Moral Principles

All four religions promote compassion, justice, and moral responsibility, though expressed in different ways.

Historical Origins and Spread

Hinduism originated in the Indian subcontinent; Judaism in the ancient Near East; Christianity from Judea; and Islam from the Arabian Peninsula.

FAQ: Common Questions About Judaism, Islam, Christianity, and Hinduism

1. What is the main difference between Judaism and Islam?

Judaism teaches a covenantal relationship between God and the Jewish people, with laws in the Torah. Islam teaches submission to Allah’s will, guided by the Qur’an and the example of Prophet Muhammad.

2. How does Christianity differ from Hinduism?

Christianity centers on faith in Jesus Christ as the Son of God and salvation through Him. Hinduism is a diverse tradition with multiple deities, reincarnation, and karma as central concepts.

3. Which is the oldest among Judaism, Islam, Christianity, and Hinduism?

Hinduism is generally considered the oldest, with roots over 3,000 years old. Judaism dates back over 2,500 years, Christianity about 2,000 years, and Islam about 1,400 years.

4. Do these religions share any common values?

Yes. All emphasize ethical living, compassion, and respect for others, though the theological foundations differ.

5. What are the primary holy books for each religion?

Judaism: Torah; Islam: Qur’an; Christianity: Bible; Hinduism: Vedas and Upanishads.
